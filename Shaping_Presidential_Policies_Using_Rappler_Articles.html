Abstract

This comprehensive study Latent Semantic Analysis (LSA) to examine the dominant themes in news articles from Rappler concerning President Bongbong Marcos' first year in office. Aimed at enhancing policy-making, strategic communications, and public engagement, the research focuses on analyzing media narratives to provide insights into public discourse and political events. The studys methodology involves collecting and processing data from Rappler, using advanced techniques like Bag-of-Words, TF-IDF, and LSA, followed by K-Means clustering to categorize themes into coherent groups. The findings reveal three primary clusters: "Political Alliances and Dynasties," "Media and Disinformation," and "International Relations and Economic Policies." These insights offer a nuanced understanding of media portrayal and public perception of political events and policies. These findings can also help in aiding in formulating strategies for policy analysis, public relations, and speech building. The study concludes by emphasizing the significance of such analytical methods in shaping effective governance and communication strategies, while recommending future research to include diverse media sources for a more balanced analysis.

Introduction

Background

One crucial aspect for political organizations and government administrations is their ability to track the publicities in the form of dominant themes of their actions and policies. One way to determine the administrations performance is to assess how they are painted in the media, may it be through opinion-heavy platforms such as social media and podcasts, or more importantly, through news sites such as Inquirer and Rappler. It has been more than one year since President Bongbong Marcos presidency, and assessing the performance other than depending on official reports such as the State of the Nation Address (SONA) can be a valuable opportunity for their administration to look into a room for improvement for their policies and platforms.

This study therefore looks into utilizing meticulous unsupervised learning methods with the purpose of encapsulating the dominant themes of the administration on various fronts, illuminating the key areas where the government has either achieved significant milestones or faced critical issues during their past year in office. By leveraging advanced methods such as Latent Semantic Analysis, this approach aims to objectively quantify and categorize the administration's actions, providing a comprehensive overview of their performance beyond the scope of traditional reporting methods.

Problem Statement

Through the use of Latent Semantic Analysis, the study aims to answer the following problem statement: what are the dominant themes and categories of Rappler news articles that can help support in crafting and assessing presidential policies? Specifically to support activities on:

  • Policy Analysis and Development. To utilize Latent Semantic Analysis (LSA) to identify and categorize dominant themes in Rappler news articles. This analysis will benefit the administration in understanding the media narrative and public discourse surrounding political events and policies.
  • Strategic Communications and Public Relations. To leverage insights from the media analysis for developing effective communication strategies for political parties, government agencies, and NGOs.
  • Speech Building and Advocacy. To identify policy areas and social issues highlighted by the media, potentially aiding in speech construction and advocacy efforts.

In conjunction with the Problem Statement, the significance of this study lies in its potential to enhance policy-making, improve strategic communications, aid in effective public engagement, and contribute to a more transparent and responsive government.

  1. Policy Analysis and Development.   2. Strategic Communications and Public Relations.   4. Scalability of the Method. It would also provide them the same aforementioned business values such as improving strategic communications and effective public engagement.

Data Sources and Description

Dataset Used

The main dataset is obtained from rappler.com wherein the search bar was utilized with the prompt of marcos year 2022 as the chosen exemplar. It will contain the first 10 pages of the search results, with each page holding 10 articles, totaling 100 rows to be utilized. The dataset will have the following variables:

  • Title string
  • Link string

This dataset will be utilized by accessing the links to the articles and creating a new dataframe containing the main content or text of each article. The new dataframe will then be subjected to the process discussed in the Methodology section

Data Assumptions and Limitations

  • Representativeness. The dataset, comprising 100 articles from Rappler obtained using the search prompt marcos year 2022, is presumed to be representative of the overall media coverage and public discourse regarding President Marcos's administration in its first year and the years leading up to the presidency. This assumption is crucial for the generalizability of the study's findings.
  • Time-Bound Analysis. Furthermore, the study focuses on articles from a specific time period, which may not fully capture the evolving nature of political discourse or the long-term trends in media coverage of the administration.
  • Scope of Data. In conjunction with the assumption of Representativeness, the dataset is confined to a single news source, Rappler, which may not capture the full diversity of media narratives or public opinion. This limitation is acknowledged in the Scalability section of the Business Value, suggesting that outcomes might vary with different news sources. This indicates a need for broader analysis to understand the administration's representation more comprehensively.
  • Stability of Online Content. The analysis is based on the assumption that the content of these articles has remained unchanged since their publication. The dataset consists of static HTML files, and any post-publication updates or edits to the articles are not considered. This assumption is important for the consistency and reliability of the data.
  • Potential Bias in Source Selection. The exclusive use of Rappler as the data source could introduce a bias in the analysis. Different news outlets may have varying editorial slants and focuses, which can shape the portrayal of political events and figures in distinct ways.

Methodology

Data Collection

Our data collection process focused on gathering information from Rappler, specifically targeting content related to the query marcos year 2022. The first ten pages of search results were downloaded as HTML files. From these files, we extracted critical information, organizing the titles, links, publication dates, and snippets of each article into a structured table. To deepen our dataset, we then accessed each link individually to retrieve the full text of the articles. This effort resulted in a comprehensive dataset, where each row represented the complete text of a single article.

Data Processing

In the data processing phase, we applied the Bag of Words model, a method crucial for converting text into numerical features, which is essential for computational models to understand and process language. This model treats each word in the document as an individual feature, disregarding grammar and word order, but maintaining frequency, which helps in understanding the prominence of certain terms and themes.

Following this, we implemented TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency), an approach particularly effective for text-heavy datasets. TF-IDF helps in distinguishing the importance of words in a document relative to a collection of documents, thus enhancing the Bag of Words model by reducing the weight of commonly used words and increasing the significance of unique terms. Finally, we applied Latent Semantic Analysis (LSA), a technique that aids in identifying patterns and relationships between terms and concepts in large text datasets. LSA was utilized as a key technique for determining the dominant themes within our dataset.

The singular vectors derived from LSA were subjected to K-Means clustering. This step was crucial for categorizing the identified themes into coherent groups, enhancing the clarity and specificity of our findings. To ensure the effectiveness of our clustering approach, we employed both the silhouette method and the Calinski-Harabasz index. The silhouette method provided a measure of how similar an object is to its own cluster compared to other clusters, offering insights into the appropriateness of the number of clusters. Meanwhile, the Calinski-Harabasz index helped in assessing the cluster validity by comparing the dispersion between and within clusters. These metrics were instrumental in fine-tuning our clustering approach, ensuring that the K-Means algorithm optimally grouped the themes identified by LSA.

Determining the Number of Singular Vectors

Using the plot of Variance Explained vs SV, we determine how much Singular Vectors we have to retain, analyze, and cluster, with the cumulative variance of 0.9.

In[13]:
# Plot the cumulative variance explained and find the number of SVs to retain
pct = 0.9
svd = TruncatedSVD(n_components=max(bow.shape))
svd.fit(bow)
var = svd.explained_variance_ratio_
n = np.where(var.cumsum() > pct)[0][0] + 1
fig, ax = plt.subplots()
ax.plot(range(1, len(var)+1), var, '-', label='individual')
ax.plot(range(1, len(var) + 1), var.cumsum(), label='cumulative')
ax.axhline(pct, ls="--", color="g")
ax.axvline(n, ls="--", color="g")
plt.xlabel("SV")
plt.ylabel('Variance Explained')
ax = ax.twinx()
ax.set_ylabel("Cumulative Variance Explained")
fig.text(
    0.5,
    -0.1,
    f"Fig 2. Variance explained vs SVs retained\n"
    f"If we want to retain a cumulative variance explained of {pct},\n"
    f"then we have to retain {n} singular vectors.",
    wrap=True,
    horizontalalignment="center",
    fontsize=12,
)

Analysis and Results

After performing K-means clustering, we arrived at three dominant clusters.

  • Cluster 0: duterte governor norte party sara bongbong presidential ilocos family vice
  • Cluster 1: media disinformation accounts comelec journalists gold duterte petition twitter family
  • Cluster 2: china singapore sona visit government state economic year philippine prices

We can call these clusters as follows:

  • Cluster 0: "Political Alliances and Dynasties"

    This cluster seems to focus on political figures, regional governance, and connections in politics. The keywords like duterte, bongbong, presidential, vice, and party suggest a focus on political personalities and their influence in regional and national politics. While the exemplar of our database explicitly mentioned marcos, we can see a strong correlation with this word to other political personalities and affiliations. Ferdinand Bongbong Marcos Jr. is paired with Sara Duterte and their alliance in the 2022 election has shown to be a formidable force after getting more than 50% of the popular votes for both presidential and vice presidential posts.

  • Cluster 1: "Media and Disinformation"

    This cluster includes keywords such as media, journalists, and twitter, pointing to the role of media and social media in the political landscape. Meanwhile, keywords like disinformation, and gold, highlight a critical threat to the integrity of media, and journalism.

  • Cluster 2: "International Relations and Economic Policies"

    Keywords like china, singapore, sona (State of the Nation Address), economic, and government suggest a focus on international relations, economic policies, and government strategies. This cluster likely covers the country's diplomatic engagements and economic initiatives, as well as their impact on local economic factors like prices.

Actionable Insights

In the political arena, the year 2022 is a turning point in the transition of presidential powers from one administration to another. Therefore, it is expected that election-related news will have its own cluster in the list of articles in our corpus given the exemplar marcos year 2022. This boils down to one of the clusters related to political alliances and dynasties. However, the second cluster has ties again with elections tackling the connection of media and disinformation with the exemplar.

With k=3 as the optimal number of clusters, two of the clusters are not directly associated with policies and programs. Thus, the stakeholder may only refer to the third cluster in terms of policy analysis and development. The researchers selected a few of the keywords from this cluster as a baseline for the insights on policy making.

Policy Analysis and Development. These keywords may reflect pressing issues and concerns that the office of the president can prioritize.

  • china Chinas Claim on the West Philippine Sea. The Philippines faces a significant challenge to its sovereignty due to China's expansive territorial claims in the West Philippine Sea. Results from dimensionality reduction indicate that this issue should be a top priority for the presidential administration. It is imperative that a comprehensive set of policies and strategies be developed to assert and secure the nations territorial rights.
  • price Increasing Prices of Basic Commodities. The rising inflation has led to a substantial increase in the prices of basic commodities that have made headlines. A notable example of this is the price of onions, which has skyrocketed to P700 per kilogram surpassing the cost of meat. This has contributed to a significant financial burden on consumers. This kind of phenomenon must be considered in influencing policies pertaining to agriculture and trade.
  • singapore International Relations and Travel. The president also made regular international trips for state visits in various locations globally with the intention of acquiring investments and grants from abroad. The result of the dimensionality reduction of news articles can be used in assessing how these travels are perceived by the media. One controversial event is the presidents travel to Singapore to attend the 2022 Grand Prix. As published in the news, critics have lambasted this move saying that this is an unnecessary expense. However, the office of the president refuted that this is to network with prospective investors for our country.

Public Relations and Image. Aside from shaping presidential policies, the results from the dimensionality reduction can be used to assess how the president is perceived by the media.

  • Proliferation of Fake News and Disinformation. From cluster 1, the Presidents image could be tainted with controversy on disinformation and fake news. They may investigate these allegations and properly clarify them through their communications team if needed.

This plan should highlight the president's active role in the execution and inauguration of key public works and infrastructure projects.

Pointers for Speech Building. By analyzing the reduced dimensions, presidential speechwriters can identify the most notable themes and topics that resonate with the public. This can be a pointer in preparation for the State of the Nation Address (SONA) where both the media and the citizens might want to hear updates on relevant and pressing issues. For example, if the dimensionality reduction reveals an emphasis on calamity resiliency, the SONA can highlight the administrations contribution to this area. If there's a significant discourse on the hike in wages, the speech can outline future initiatives to meet this clamor.

Conclusion

Using Latent Semantic Analysis (LSA) to analyze Rappler news articles, this study has identified key themes and narratives that surround President Bongbong Marcos' first year in office. The three identified clusters - "Political Alliances and Dynasties," "Media and Disinformation," and "International Relations and Economic Policies" - provide a multifaceted view of the current political and economic climate that the Marcos administration is facing. The utilization of LSA and clustering as a tool has been pivotal in this study to objectively discern these dominant themes, which demonstrated its efficacy in extracting meaningful insights from complex media narratives.

The insights mentioned provide an assessment of how the administration's image and actions are being perceived and reported. This information is particularly valuable for the Presidential Communications Office and other stakeholders in crafting strategies for policy analysis and development, public relations, and speech building. The findings suggest potential areas where the administration may need to focus its efforts, such as addressing issues related to combating disinformation and managing international relations and economic policies effectively.

In conclusion, the study not only sheds light on the current state of political and economic affairs under the Marcos administration but also highlights the importance of advanced analytical methods like LSA in shaping effective governance and communication strategies.

Recommendation

It is important to acknowledge the limitations of this study, primarily the reliance on a single news source. This may introduce certain biases in the portrayal of the administration. Future research could expand the scope to include a broader range of media organizations to make our database more balanced. In addition, continuous monitoring and analysis using LSA could help in tracking the evolving nature of media narratives, thereby enabling more dynamic and responsive policy-making and communication strategies.
